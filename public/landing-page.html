Voca.ai and Carnegie Mellon University partner to enable fast diagnosis of COVID-19

The project, Corona Voice Detect, was created for early diagnosis of COVID-19 (Coronavirus). By combining deep learning and voice forensic technologies, in order to find patterns in voice and tone, determining unique illnesses.

But first we need your help! We need to curate recordings in order for Corona Voice Detect to be ready for consumer use. Make your mark and become a part of this historic journey by helping us diagnose and slow the spreading of Coronavirus.

Corona Voice Detect, is led by Dr. Shmuel Ur a renown inventor and Dr. Alan Bekker, CTO & Co-founder at Voca.ai. The success of this project will have an immense impact on the health of our communities and prevention in spreading. 

We’re starting with global data collection, using deep learning to develop trends in speech recognition. With this data we are able to define the ailments hidden within speech, tone and voice to determine the probability of being infected with the virus. Professor Rita Singh from Carnegie Mellon University along with other researchers, will use this data to contribute to and develop an algorithm detecting illness in voice and diagnosis of Coronavirus.

Following the success of the data collection phase, people will soon be able to determine the likelihood of contracting the virus – with just one short voice recording.
